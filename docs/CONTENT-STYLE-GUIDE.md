# Content Style Guide

## Voice

Professional American English: clear, calm, specific, knowledgeable, B2B, healthcare-aware. We write like a competent operator explaining things to a smart physician-owner — never like a brochure. Short paragraphs, descriptive headings, useful tables and checklists, one H1 per page.

## The Honesty Rules (non-negotiable)

Never state or imply: years of experience, client counts, revenue collected, collection improvements, claim-acceptance percentages, certifications, certified-coder staffing (unless contractually true and scoped), staff size, a Texas office address, US-employee claims, case studies, testimonials, software/payer partnerships, association memberships, awards, HIPAA/SOC 2/BBB certification, client logos, reviews, company-attributed statistics, or guaranteed financial results.

Allowed and encouraged: industry figures **attributed as industry observations** with hedged language matching evidence ("industry surveys consistently put...", "commonly observed in the X–Y range"), process commitments we actually make, and disclosed perspective ("we sell billing services and say so").

“HIPAA certified” is banned absolutely — the site explains why on /hipaa-and-security/. Compliance claims are framed as implemented safeguards subject to owner verification.

## Banned Vocabulary

Revolutionize, game-changing, world-class, unparalleled, cutting-edge, seamless, unlock, transform-your-practice-overnight, maximize-revenue-guaranteed, number-one/best medical billing company, industry-leading (unless sourced), trusted-by-thousands (unless true). Also banned: fake urgency, fake scarcity, countdown anything.

## Disclaimer Requirements

- Educational disclaimer (config DISCLAIMER): footer sitewide; inline on guides, specialty coding sections, payer pages
- Payer pages: PAYER_DISCLAIMER (non-affiliation), rendered automatically by the template
- Software pages: SOFTWARE_DISCLAIMER (independence), rendered automatically
- Forms: PHI_WARNING beside every form, rendered by LeadForm
- Solutions/services: no-guarantee language where outcomes are discussed
- Pricing content: ranges framed as industry observation, never company quotes

## Differentiation Standard

Every programmatic entry must contain type-specific substance that could not be produced by swapping a keyword into a sibling entry. Test: cover the title — can you tell which specialty/city/service this is from the body? If not, rewrite.

## Texas Facts Standard

City/market claims restricted to well-established, verifiable facts (major institutions, industries, broad payer-mix characterizations). No invented statistics, no fake local flavor, no implied local offices — the remote-service phrasing ("serving practices in X through secure remote workflows") is mandatory on location pages.

## Formatting Conventions

Title Case for page titles and H1s; sentence case acceptable in body headings where it reads better. Em dashes spaced per site convention. Numbers: figures for metrics and money. FAQ answers: complete, useful, 2–5 sentences — never one-line brush-offs.

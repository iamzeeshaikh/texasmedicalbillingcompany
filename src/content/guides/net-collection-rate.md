## Definition

Net collection rate measures collections against what you were contractually entitled to collect: payments ÷ (charges − contractual adjustments), over a matched period. Where gross collection rate compares payments to billed charges (a number distorted by fee schedule markup), net collection rate asks the honest question — of the money actually collectible under your contracts, how much did you get?

## Why It Matters

It is the closest thing to a single grade for revenue cycle performance. Denials written off, balances abandoned, underpayments absorbed, timely-filing losses — every failure mode lands in the gap between 100% and your rate. A practice can have fast days in AR and clean claims and still leak here, because this metric catches what the others excuse.

## Benchmarks

Well-run practices commonly land in the 95–99% range. Below the mid-90s, real money is dying somewhere in the cycle — the diagnostic question is where, answered by the write-off and adjustment detail behind the number.

## Computing It Honestly

The trap is the denominator: "contractual adjustments" must contain only true contract differences (billed rate minus allowed amount), not operational losses disguised as adjustments. Practices that post denials, timely-filing losses, and unworked balances as "contractual" manufacture flattering rates that measure nothing. Adjustment-code discipline — every code with a defined meaning, operational write-offs separated from contractual ones — is a precondition for the metric meaning anything.

Use matched periods (payments lag charges, so trailing 12-month windows smooth the distortion) and hold the formula constant.

## Reading a Low Rate

Decompose the write-off detail by category: denial write-offs point to prevention and appeal gaps; timely-filing losses point to submission and follow-up process; small-balance purges point to workflow economics; bad-debt patient balances point to front-end estimates and statement discipline. Underpayments absorbed silently will not appear at all — pairing this metric with paid-versus-contract variance analysis completes the picture.

## Common Errors

- Operational losses miscoded as contractual adjustments, inflating the rate
- Unmatched periods making the rate swing with growth
- Celebrating the rate while never auditing what the adjustment categories contain
- Confusing it with gross collection rate in benchmarking conversations

## Practical Checklist

- [ ] Adjustment codes documented, with contractual and operational categories separated
- [ ] Trailing-12-month computation on a fixed formula
- [ ] Write-off detail reviewed by category quarterly
- [ ] Paired with underpayment variance checks
- [ ] Reported monthly beside days in AR and denial rate

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Our net collection rate is 98% — are we fine?**
Probably good, verify honestly: if operational losses are being posted as contractual adjustments, the 98% is decorative. Audit a sample of adjustments against contracts; a genuine 98% on disciplined posting is a rate worth trusting.

**What is a realistic improvement pace?**
The rate moves as its causes fix: denial prevention and follow-up discipline show within one to two quarters; posting-discipline corrections may make the rate briefly look worse (as hidden losses surface) before it becomes truthfully better. Expect measured movement, not miracles.
